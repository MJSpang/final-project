Aretha Franklin

Queen of Soul

  • About

    Was born on March 25, 1942 in Memphis, Tennessee to parents Barbara and Clarence LaVaugn in there family home. Her mother was a piano player and vocalist while her father was a minister and preacher.

  • Music

    Aretha taught herself how to play piano by ear and at this time she would start her music career at her father's church in Detroit. Her father would then start to bring her on "gospel caravan" tours.

  • Civil Rights

    Franklin was both involved in civil rights and women's rights growing. She would provide money for civil rights groups, as well would at times give money for their pay roll, an performed at protests.

Franklin sang “I Dreamed A Dream” from Les Misérables at the Presidential Gala at the Capital Centre in Landover, Maryland for Bill Clinton's inaguration. Franklin and her chorus transformed the song into an emotional, uplifting ballad.

January 2009

Aretha sang “My Country, ‘Tis of Thee” at Barack Obama’s inauguration in 2009. She’d stood on the steps in freezing cold in a gray hat, her voice sailing over the huge crowd that stretched back to the Reflecting Pool.
